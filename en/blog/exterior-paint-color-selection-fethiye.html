    Introduction

    The combination of high UV and relative humidity in Fethiye directly affects the fading rate of colors and surface temperature. An aesthetic and durable facade is achieved with the correct LRV range and finish selection.

    Color Science: LRV & UV

    LRV (Light Reflectance Value) is a value ranging from %0 (black) ‚Äì %100 (white). As LRV increases, the surface heats less, UV-induced fading and coating fatigue decrease. For comfort and durability in coastal climate, the LRV %55‚Äì85 range is ideal.

    LRV range and recommended usage
    LRVRecommendationNote
    %20‚Äì40Dark accentsCaution if facing south; heat load increases.
    %55‚Äì70Main facadeGood heat/UV balance, moderate dirt visibility.
    %70‚Äì85Large surfaceLowest heating; dirt visibility decreases.

    Palette by Facade Orientation

    • South/West: Light beige, off-white, light stone tones. Dark colors only in small areas like niches/frames.
    • North: One tone warmer/neutral; since light is less, pastel warm grays work well.
    • East: Soft sand and wheat tones; appear balanced in morning light.

    Texture & Finish

    Siloxane/silicone-based exterior paints provide vapor permeability + dirt repellency. Medium-grained decorative plasters reduce the visibility of micro cracks. Maintain tone harmony with UV-resistant varnish/paint on metal/wood accents.

    Sample & Test

    1. Test selected 2‚Äì3 tones as 1‚Äì2 m¬≤ samples on south and west facades.
    2. Compare colors at midday and evening hours; evaluate by eye, not camera.
    3. Stay light-toned in dirt-prone areas (eave underside, rain marks).

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Can I use dark colors?

    Yes, but prefer low LRV tones on small surfaces and shaded facades.

    How do I find the LRV value?

    It's on the product color chart/technical datasheet. Otherwise, manufacturer/dealer provides support.

    Is sample required?

    Yes. Testing 1‚Äì2 m¬≤ in real light on south and west facades guarantees the final tone.
