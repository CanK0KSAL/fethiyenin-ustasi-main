    Introduction

    When choosing wall coating, two popular alternatives stand out: stone plaster (mineral-based, high durability) and wall fabric (textile/cellulose-based decorative coating). Making the right choice according to your aesthetic, acoustic and maintenance expectations directly affects total cost and satisfaction.

    Quick Comparison

    Comparison table of stone plaster and wall fabric
    FeatureStone PlasterWall Fabric
    Durability High surface hardness, resistant to impact and scratching Medium; easy local repair on point damage
    Acoustic Reduces reflection but limited Increases acoustic comfort thanks to texture structure
    Moisture Resistance Good with suitable primer; more resistant to mold/stains Vapor permeable; product selection critical in excessive humidity
    Maintenance/Repair May require sanding & touch-up; professional intervention Local repair and piece renewal easier
    Visual Impact Natural stone texture, premium perception Warm, textile feel; pattern/texture variety
    Application Speed Medium with inter-layer drying times Fast; within the same day according to area size

    Where Which One?

    • Entry hall / corridor: High impact and friction ‚Üí Stone plaster stands out.
    • Bedroom / TV wall: Acoustic and warm texture ‚Üí Wall fabric more suitable.
    • Coastal climate (√áalƒ±≈ü, G√∂cek): If intense humidity/UV, correct primer and product selection required.
    • High tenant turnover apartment: Wall fabric for those who want easy renewal.
    • Accent wall & premium appearance: Continuity and depth with stone plaster.
    Pro tip: Combining wall fabric in TV unit and niches with wood panel strengthens both acoustics and visual layer.

    Application & Maintenance

    1. Surface Preparation: Remove weak layers of old paint/plaster, clean surface from dust, apply suitable primer.
    2. Stone Plaster: Follow inter-layer drying times; check continuity at joints and corners under light.
    3. Wall Fabric: Mix/rest according to product instructions; hide seams with pattern.
    4. Maintenance: Wipe with damp cloth on stone plaster; soft cloth on wall fabric and do stain test at invisible point.
    Warning: Application on humid surface and insufficient primer increases risk of bubbling/staining. Product selection critical on UV-exposed surfaces.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Which is safer in humid climate?

    With correct primer and product, stone plaster is more tolerant; in wall fabric, product selection and ventilation are critical.

    Your recommendation for homes with children?

    Stone plaster in impact areas like corridor/entry; wall fabric for acoustic in bedroom/study area.

    If I want to change color later?

    On stone plaster, coating with paint is possible; in wall fabric, local removal-installation or product-on-top application options can be evaluated.
