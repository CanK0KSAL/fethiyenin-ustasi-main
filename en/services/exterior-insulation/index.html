    Layers & Details

    Sheathing Layers

    1. Adhesive + mechanical anchoring.
    2. Panel joint staggering and expansion tracking.
    3. Mesh + corner profile + drip edge profile.
    4. Thin render + primer + final coat paint/coating.

    Critical Points

    • Window-door sills and drip edges.
    • Roof eave/water downpipe surroundings.
    • Thermal bridge risk columns-beams, balconies.

    Repair & Reinforcement

    • Local repair mortar on peeling/cracked surfaces.
    • Biocidal cleaning + protection for mold/algae.
    • Rule of leaving sound substrate on old paint.

    Water Management

    • Drip edge continuity and sill slopes.
    • Elastic sealant in vertical joints.

    Materials & Performance

    Panel Selection

    • EPS: Economical, good thermal insulation, lightweight.
    • XPS: Low water absorption, high impact resistance.
    • Mineral Wool: Fire resistance and sound insulation.

    Connection & Mesh

    • Anchor model/count according to wall type.
    • Glass fiber mesh weight and overlap allowances.

    Coating & Paint

    • Acrylic/silicone binder paints (vapor permeable).
    • Silicone/acrylic render; texture control with grain size.
    • Stainless anchor and ventilation gap in stone/composite.

    Durability

    • UV, rain, wind loads and expansion compatibility.
    • Color tone (L* value) and heating effect evaluation.

    Weather & Application Window

    Conditions

    • Application not performed during precipitation and strong wind.
    • Frost risk and excessive heat/direct sun avoided.
    • Surface moisture and dew point checked.

    Site Organization

    • Scaffolding safety and netting.
    • Neighbor/ground protection tarps.
    • Daily application area and curing time plan.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Which panel should I prefer for sheathing?

    According to need: EPS (economy), XPS (low water absorption), Mineral Wool (fire/acoustic). We provide recommendations based on building conditions.

    My existing paint is peeling; how does renewal work?

    Weak layers removed, corrected with repair mortar; renewed with proper primer and exterior paint.

    Do you work in rain?

    No; application not performed if there is precipitation, strong wind and frost risk. Drying times are observed.

    Do you provide warranty?

    We provide workmanship warranty; paints and coatings are subject to manufacturer warranty conditions.

    Will color fade?

    Risk reduced with high UV resistant products; we share technical warnings for dark and high heat-retaining tones.
